EVIDENCE:
Recovery investigations frequently generate large runtime captures, logs, dumps, inspections, and temporary evidence files.

Over time these artifacts accumulate rapidly and become difficult to search, maintain, and govern.

The valuable asset is the certified truth derived from the recovery process rather than the raw artifact itself.

TRUTH:
Recovery artifacts exist to preserve certified truth.

Recovery artifacts are not archival storage.

MEANING:
The purpose of recovery evidence is to extract reusable institutional knowledge.

Raw recovery artifacts should not become a permanent substitute for certified discoveries.

RULE:
Any recovery artifact exceeding:

10 MB

OR

5,000 lines

must be summarized into a DISC artifact and the raw capture deleted after verification.
